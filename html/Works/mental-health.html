Up & Up

Helping Georgia Tech students relieve their stress through collective breathing games


Overview

Objective

Being in a well-established school, a majority of Georgia Tech students are typically known as Type A students, who are more achievement-oriented, ambitious, and competitive. The heavy workload at Tech, together with students' high standards for themselves, leads to a high level of stress and other mental health issues. Recently, one Georgia Tech student who suffered from depression unfortunately passed away. The incident prompted the administration to invest 1 million dollars into mental health initiatives aimed to lower students' stress level.

Within such a context, we designed an intervention that helps deal with student stress. Our design motivates students to slow down and perform breathing exercises together with other members in the community.

My Role

UX Designer, UX Researcher

Timeline

Sept. 2017 - Dec. 2017

Team Members

Tony Jin, Jessica Tsui, Dongwhi Yoo

Tools

Sketch, Principle



Methods

We first conducted a literature review on stress and an expert interview with a mental health expert. The information we gathered, together with the feedback we received from classmates and professors, helped us settle on topic of promoting collective breathing as a way to reduce student stress.

We then conducted a literature review on breathing exercises, and three competitive analyses on existing apps that promote breathing exercises, existing apps that provide community-based health solutions, and sensors that can measure breathing patterns. We also conducted interviews with potential users, and created two persona to guide our design process...
